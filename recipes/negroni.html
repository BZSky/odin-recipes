The Negroni

Description

Ah, Negroni! The italian classic cocktail that never tastes the same, despite having an easy recipe. Glowing red, the Negroni manages to be both sophisticated and simple at the same time and is definitely for a grown-up palate - for many it's simply too bitter. It takes its depth from the vermouth, is centred by the bittersweet liqueur, and is made to sing through the vitality of the gin. It makes the perfect aperitivo and, though popular the world over, is the traditional default during aperitivo hour in Milan.
Hence, the gin and vermouth you use can transform the taste of your Negroni significantly.

Ingredients

  • London Dry Gin 1 fl oz
  • Campari bitter liqueur 1 fl oz
  • Italian rosso (red) vermouth 1 fl oz
  • Always garnish with an orange zest twist or orange slice

Steps

  1. Add all ingredients to a mixing glass with ice and stir to mix
  2. Add ice to an Old Fashioned glass
  3. STIR all ingredients with ice for 20-40 revolutions (a drink should be cool but a bit on a boozy side to prolong dilution in the glass)
  4. Strain into ice-filled glass (preferably over a large cube or chunk of block ice)
  5. Garnish with an Orange slice (author preferred) or an orange zest twist
